Research

I'm interested in Human-Computer Interaction, Ubiquitous Computing and AI for Cyber-Physical Sensing. Most of my research focuses on sensing human behaviors through ubiquitous data sources, such as wireless signals, eye-tracking data, and speech. Recently, I have been exploring the use of large language models (LLMs) to assist in sensing and interpreting human interaction behaviors.

SigCan: Toward Reliable ToF Estimation Leveraging Multipath Signal Cancellation on Commodity WiFi Devices
Yang Li, Dan Wu, Jiahe Chen, Weiyan Shi, Leye Wang, Lu Su, Wenwei Li, Daqing Zhang*
IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing, 2024
paper

A phase difference-based method for ToF estimation is proposed, effectively mitigating multipath and noise effects and achieving significant accuracy improvements in distance estimation with WiFi devices.

Contrastive learning for knowledge-based question generation in large language models
Zhenhong Zhang, Jiajing Chen, Weiyan Shi, Lingjie Yi, Chihang Wang, Qian Yu

arXiv

An enhanced question generation method using contrastive learning is proposed to improve accuracy and reduce hallucinations in knowledge-based systems, demonstrating significant gains in question quality through contrasting context and chain-of-thought prompts.

Exploring Gaze Pattern in Autistic Children: Clustering, Visualization, and Prediction
Weiyan Shi, Haihong Zhang*, Jin Yang, Ruiqing Ding, YongWei Zhu, Kenny Tsu Wei Choo
arXiv

A novel method is proposed to automatically analyze gaze behaviors in ASD children, using optimized clustering and predictive models to achieve promising prediction performance in gaze-based ASD diagnosis.

Understanding the Diffraction Model in Static Multipath-Rich Environments for WiFi Sensing System Design
Xuanzhi Wang, Anlan Yu, Kai Niu, Weiyan Shi, Junzhe Wang, Zhiyun Yao, Rahul C Shah, Hong Lu, Daqing Zhang
IEEE Transactions on Mobile Computing, 2024
paper

An enhanced diffraction model is proposed to improve WiFi-based sensing in the diffraction zone by accounting for CSI amplitude, phase, and multipath, achieving over 96% accuracy in intrusion detection and 0.6 bpm error in respiration monitoring.

WiCross: I Can Know When You Cross Using COTS WiFi Devices
Weiyan Shi, Xuanzhi Wang, Kai Niu, Leye Wang, Daqing Zhang
UbiComp/ISWC Adjunct, 2023
paper

WiCross is a WiFi-based system that accurately detects crossing behaviors by distinguishing turn-back actions with phase statistics, achieving over 95% accuracy and a false alarm rate below 5% in smart home applications.
